"A father's love does not end with his departure.
It learns a different language — spoken in quiet mornings,
in unexpected strength, in the part of his daughter
that simply refuses to break."

There are no words adequate for the particular weight of losing a father. What I believe — with everything I am — is that the love a father pours into his child does not evaporate when he leaves. It becomes something more quiet and more permanent: a warmth that arrives on the coldest days, a steadiness that appears in your spine when the world asks too much of you.

Uncle was not just your father. He was your first witness — the first person in this world who looked at you and thought: this one is something rare. The compassion you carry, the grace with which you love people freely, the quiet resilience that lives just beneath your surface — that is his legacy. Walking. Breathing. Glowing. In you.
